Business Case:

Bellabeat wants to gain a greater foothold in the wearable fitness tracker marketplace by understanding how women use competing products. The company is interested in usage trends for competing products. They would like to apply the findings from this analysis to one of their existing products to drive sales and increase customer base.

01: FitBit Fitness Tracker Data: A public data set containing continuous output for a range of bio metrics, courtesy of Mobius.

Visuals and Findings

When comparing steps taken to sedentary minutes, it appears that there isnt necessarily an inverse relationship between the two. The data shows that during more sedentary days, users still often track roughly similar steps as less sedentary days, suggesting that a lot of people likely have long periods of inactivity with bouts of movement in between. The data point coloring shows that rather than have two main clusters of separate users, each users tends to have varying activity.

ggplot(data=activity_fixed, aes(x=TotalSteps, y=SedentaryMinutes, color=Id)) + geom_point()

The time that users spend in bed correlates fairly well with the amount of sleep they achieved. There are some notable groupings that stand out from this linear progression, however. The standouts are consistent in that the time they spend in bed equates to less than the average amount of sleep. It is likely that these divergent data points are due to either struggles to sleep or lounging. Here, we see that the deviant data points are from a particular subset of users as identified by their ID colors. These users likely have a harder time falling asleep or different nighttime routines than the main userbase.

ggplot(data=sleep_fixed, aes(x=TotalMinutesAsleep, y=TotalTimeInBed, color=Id)) + geom_point()

Next, total times at different activity levels will be analyzed to determine exactly how FitBit users are splitting their activity levels. By taking the mean of each activity level: very active, fairly active, and lightly active, we can get a breakdown of the general users habits. Through the bar graph, we can see that the vast majority of active minutes is spent in the Lightly Active category. In fact, for the generalized user, the very and moderately active categories combined equate to less than an hour out of the users day. A potential inference from this summary is that, while most FitBit users may be health and fitness conscious, may are not performing athletic activity with any consistency.

According to a study done by the American College of Rheumatology and their communications with Fitabase (a popular platform for exporting fitness data), FitBits criteria for activity is derived from calculations performed on metabolic equivalent tasks (METs). A MET, another study done on its relationship with exercise, is defined as the amount of oxygen consumed while sitting at rest and is equal to 3.5 ml O2 per kg body weight x min.

  • Semanik, P., Lee, J., Pellegrini, C.A., Song, J., Dunlop, D.D. and Chang, R.W. (2020), Comparison of Physical Activity Measures Derived From the Fitbit Flex and the ActiGraph GT3X+ in an Employee Population With Chronic Knee Symptoms. ACR Open Rheumatology, 2: 48-52. https://doi.org/10.1002/acr2.11099
  • Jett M, Sidney K, Blmchen G. Metabolic equivalents (METS) in exercise testing, exercise prescription, and evaluation of functional capacity. Clin Cardiol. 1990 Aug;13(8):555-65. doi: 10.1002/clc.4960130809. PMID: 2204507.
